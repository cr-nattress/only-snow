## What Makes It Different

### Compared to OpenSnow
OpenSnow is a weather app for ski resorts. You pick a resort, then check its forecast. OnlySnow flips this: you tell us your constraints (location, pass, drive time), and we tell you which resort wins today. OpenSnow answers *"What's the weather at Vail?"* â€” OnlySnow answers *"Should I go to Vail, Breck, or Keystone?"*

### Compared to resort apps
Resort apps show conditions for one mountain. OnlySnow compares every mountain you can reach and ranks them against each other. It also surfaces resorts you weren't considering.

### Compared to generic weather apps
Weather apps show weather. OnlySnow shows decisions. It knows your pass, your drive time, your kids' ages, and your travel willingness. It turns raw forecast data into *"Leave by 5 AM, drive to Breck, ski Peak 6."*

## The Data Behind It

OnlySnow aggregates data from multiple sources to power its recommendations:

- **Snow forecasts** â€” Updated daily, covering 10-day windows for every resort
- **Current conditions** â€” Base depth, lifts open, trails open, grooming status; updated every 6 hours

## Design Philosophy

**1. Never cry wolf.** If there's no snow, the app stays quiet. No filler content, no "conditions are fair" alerts. When OnlySnow notifies you, it means something.

**2. Answer, don't inform.** Every screen answers a question. The dashboard answers "where should I ski?" The chase page answers "where's the biggest storm?" The alerts answer "do I need to act right now?" Raw data is always translated into a recommendation.

**3. Respect the 5 AM decision.** Most ski day decisions happen before sunrise. The app is built for bleary-eyed, one-thumb scrolling. Big numbers, clear rankings, obvious winners. No scrolling through paragraphs to find the answer.

**4. Your pass matters.** Knowing a non-pass resort got more snow is useful context, but leading with it would be cruel. OnlySnow always leads with your pass resorts, and only surfaces off-pass options when the difference is dramatic.

**5. Two alerts per day, max.** Notification fatigue kills apps. OnlySnow enforces a hard limit and scores every potential alert by urgency, relevance, and recency before choosing which two to send.
