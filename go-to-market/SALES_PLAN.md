## Executive Summary

OnlySnow's sales strategy focuses on three revenue channels: (1) **B2C Premium Subscriptions** via product-led growth with in-app conversion, (2) **B2B Resort Partnerships** via direct enterprise sales to independent ski resorts, and (3) **B2B2C Pass Provider Partnerships** with Epic, Ikon, and Indy Pass for white-label or co-marketing deals. This plan outlines our sales process, target accounts, pricing strategy, and Year 1 revenue targets.

**Year 1 Revenue Target:** $525,000
- Premium Subscriptions: $400,000 (5,000 users √ó $80/year)
- Resort Partnerships: $50,000 (10 partners √ó $5K/year)
- Affiliate Commissions: $75,000 (500 destination trips √ó $150 avg commission)

### 2.2 Pricing Strategy

**Monthly Plan:** $9.99/month
- Target: Casual users who want to "try before they buy"
- Positioned as "less than a latte per month"

**Annual Plan:** $79/year ($6.58/month, save 34%)
- Target: Core users who ski 10+ days/season
- Positioned as "cheaper than one lift ticket"

**Family Plan:** $129/year (up to 4 accounts)
- Target: Family planners coordinating trips
- Positioned as "$2.60/person/month"

**Pricing Rationale:**
- **Competitive Benchmark:** OpenSnow Premium is $99/year; OnlySnow is $79 (20% cheaper)
- **Value-Based Pricing:** If OnlySnow saves 1 hour of trip planning per storm (5 storms/season), that's 5 hours saved. At $50/hr value of time, that's $250 value for $79 price = 3.1x ROI
- **Affordability:** $79 is 5-10% of average season pass cost ($700-1,500), making it a "no-brainer" upsell

### 2.3 Conversion Funnel

**Stage 1: Awareness ‚Üí Signup**
- Channel: Marketing campaigns, word-of-mouth, App Store
- Conversion target: 20% of website visitors sign up
- Friction: Email/Google OAuth signup (no credit card required)

**Stage 2: Signup ‚Üí Activation**
- Definition: User completes onboarding (sets location, selects pass, chooses drive radius)
- Conversion target: 80% of signups activate
- Tactics: In-app prompts, onboarding email sequence

**Stage 3: Activation ‚Üí Engaged User**
- Definition: User opens app 3+ times in first week
- Conversion target: 60% of activated users become engaged
- Tactics: Push notifications (first storm alert), email reminders

**Stage 4: Engaged User ‚Üí Premium Subscriber**
- Definition: User pays for monthly or annual Premium plan
- Conversion target: 10% of engaged users convert to Premium
- Tactics: In-app paywalls, limited-time offers, feature gating

**Funnel Math (Year 1):**
- Website visitors: 80,000
- Signups (20%): 16,000
- Activated (80%): 12,800
- Engaged (60%): 7,680
- Premium (10%): 768 per month avg ‚Üí **5,000 annual Premium users**

### 2.4 In-App Conversion Tactics

**Paywall Trigger Points (When to Show Premium Upsell):**

1. **After 3 "Worth Knowing" Dismissals**
   - Message: "You've dismissed 3 opportunities for better snow. Unlock Premium to see regional alerts up to 6 hours away."
   - CTA: "Unlock Regional Discovery"

2. **When User Taps Chase Mode**
   - Message: "Chase Mode is for destination travelers. Get national storm tracking + AI trip planning."
   - CTA: "Unlock Chase Mode ‚Äì $79/year"

3. **After 5 Powder Day Alerts (Free Tier Gets 1 Resort Only)**
   - Message: "Your other resorts are getting powder too. Unlock alerts for all your resorts."
   - CTA: "Upgrade to Premium"

4. **When Storm Severity Reaches "Epic" or "Chase" Level**
   - Message: "This is a chase-worthy storm. Unlock full trip planning to book your trip."
   - CTA: "Get Your Trip Plan ‚Äì Upgrade Now"

5. **After 30 Days of Active Use (Habit Formation)**
   - Message: "You've checked OnlySnow 20 times this month. Get the most out of it with Premium."
   - CTA: "Upgrade for $6.58/month"

**Paywall Best Practices:**
- **Timing:** Show paywall after user experiences value (not on Day 1)
- **Value Prop:** Show specific benefit user is missing ("Unlock Chase Mode"), not generic "Upgrade"
- **Social Proof:** "Join 2,500+ Premium users who never miss a powder day"
- **Urgency:** "Limited-time offer: Save 50% on your first year" (seasonal sale)

### 2.5 Seasonal Pricing Strategy

**High Season (Nov - Mar): Full Price**
- Pricing: $9.99/month, $79/year
- Rationale: Demand is high, users are actively skiing

**Shoulder Season (Apr - May, Sep - Oct): 25% Off**
- Pricing: $7.49/month, $59/year
- Rationale: Encourage early renewals and off-season retention

**Low Season (Jun - Aug): 50% Off**
- Pricing: $4.99/month, $39/year
- Rationale: Acquire users before high season, build waitlist for next winter

**First-Time User Offer: 7-Day Free Trial**
- Access: Full Premium features for 7 days
- Conversion: Auto-convert to paid after 7 days (opt-out, not opt-in)
- Timing: Offer during onboarding ("Try Premium free for 7 days")

## 3. B2B Resort Partnerships (Direct Sales)

### 3.1 Target Account Profile

**Ideal Customer Profile (ICP):**
- **Size:** Independent or regional ski resorts (100K-500K annual visitors)
- **Geography:** Western U.S. (CO, UT, WY, ID, MT, NM, CA, OR, WA)
- **Challenge:** Competing with mega-resorts (Vail Resorts, Alterra) for visibility
- **Budget:** Marketing budget $50K-500K/year, willing to spend $5K-20K on partnerships

**Tiering System:**

**Tier 1 (High Priority):** Independent mountains with strong local followings
- Examples: Monarch Mountain, Loveland, Silverton, Bridger Bowl, Taos, Sunlight, Powder Mountain
- Pain Point: "Epic/Ikon resorts get all the visibility, even when we have better snow"
- OnlySnow Value: "Worth Knowing" discovery when they outperform nearby resorts

**Tier 2 (Medium Priority):** Regional darlings with destination appeal
- Examples: Schweitzer, Mt. Bachelor, Purgatory, Big Sky (independent), Crested Butte (before Vail acquisition)
- Pain Point: "We're a hidden gem, but destination travelers default to Vail/Park City"
- OnlySnow Value: National chase alerts + trip planning drive fly-in traffic

**Tier 3 (Lower Priority):** Mega-resorts (Epic/Ikon)
- Examples: Vail, Breckenridge, Park City, Aspen
- Pain Point: "We don't need discovery, but we want first-party data on user behavior"
- OnlySnow Value: Data licensing (how many OnlySnow users visit, where they come from, what they care about)

## 4. B2B2C Pass Provider Partnerships (Strategic Deals)

### 4.1 Target Partners

**Tier 1: Epic Pass (Vail Resorts)**
- **Pass Holders:** 2.3M
- **Avg Pass Price:** $900
- **Opportunity:** License OnlySnow's recommendation engine for Epic Pass app
- **Pitch:** "Increase pass renewals by 5% by showing pass holders personalized resort recommendations"
- **Revenue Model:** $500K licensing fee or 5% rev share on incremental pass sales

**Tier 2: Ikon Pass (Alterra Mountain Company)**
- **Pass Holders:** 2M
- **Avg Pass Price:** $1,000
- **Opportunity:** Co-marketing to Ikon Pass email list + API integration
- **Pitch:** "Drive more visits to underutilized resorts (e.g., Tremblant, Deer Valley) by surfacing them when conditions are best"
- **Revenue Model:** $300K licensing fee or affiliate commission on Ikon Pass sales via OnlySnow

**Tier 3: Indy Pass**
- **Pass Holders:** 500K
- **Avg Pass Price:** $300
- **Opportunity:** Exclusive partnership (Indy Pass optimization in OnlySnow app)
- **Pitch:** "Help Indy Pass holders maximize their 2-days-per-resort limit by ranking resorts by snow forecast"
- **Revenue Model:** Rev share on Indy Pass sales driven by OnlySnow (5% of $300 = $15 per sale)

### 4.2 Partnership Approach

**Phase 1: Relationship Building (Year 1)**

**Goal:** Get on their radar, build trust

**Tactics:**
1. **Attend industry conferences:** NSAA, SIA Snow Show
2. **Request introductory meetings:** LinkedIn outreach to VP of Digital, VP of Marketing
3. **Offer free value:** "We've analyzed OnlySnow user behavior for Epic Pass holders. Here's what we learned."
4. **Press coverage:** Get featured in Ski Magazine, TechCrunch ‚Üí Epic/Ikon will notice

**Phase 2: Pilot Program (Year 2)**

**Goal:** Prove value with small pilot

**Tactics:**
1. **API integration:** OnlySnow displays real-time blackout dates, pass benefits from Epic/Ikon API
2. **Co-marketing test:** Epic/Ikon sends one email to 10K pass holders promoting OnlySnow
3. **Attribution tracking:** Measure incremental pass sales, resort visits, user engagement

**Phase 3: Full Partnership (Year 3)**

**Goal:** White-label license or exclusive partnership

**Tactics:**
1. **Licensing deal:** Epic/Ikon licenses OnlySnow's recommendation engine for their app
2. **Acquisition:** Vail Resorts or Alterra acquires OnlySnow outright ($10M-50M exit)

### 4.3 Strategic Value of Pass Partnerships

**Why Epic/Ikon Should Partner:**
1. **Increase pass renewals:** Users who ski more days/season are more likely to renew (OnlySnow drives 2-3 extra ski days per user)
2. **Distribute traffic:** Epic/Ikon want to spread crowds across all resorts, not just Vail/Breckenridge
3. **Data insights:** OnlySnow tracks user behavior (which resorts are preferred, when they ski, what conditions they seek)
4. **Competitive moat:** If Epic partners with OnlySnow exclusively, Ikon pass holders can't access it

**Why OnlySnow Should Partner:**
1. **Distribution:** Instant access to 2.3M Epic Pass holders or 2M Ikon Pass holders
2. **Credibility:** "Official Partner of Epic Pass" badge builds trust
3. **Revenue:** Licensing fees or rev share provide non-dilutive capital
4. **Data access:** API integration with Epic/Ikon's systems (real-time lift status, crowd levels)
